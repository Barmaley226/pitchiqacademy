Dribbling + Acceleration Drill

Session Length: 5â€“10 minutes

Age Group: All ages

Skills Developed: Close control, foot speed, coordination, acceleration, stamina, quick direction changes

Equipment: 1 ball, 10 cones

Space Needed: 10â€“25 meters (depending on sprint distance)

Setup

  • Place 4 cones in a zigzag pattern with about 2 meters between each cone.
  • After the zigzag section, place another 4-5 cones in a straight line for a basic cone weave, spaced about 2-4 meters apart.
  • Optionally mark a 10â€“20 meter sprint zone after the last cone.

Instructions

  1. Start with the ball at the first zigzag cone.
  2. Dribble through the 4-cone zigzag, changing direction quickly at each cone.
  3. Continue directly into the basic cone weave, using tight, controlled touches.
  4. After the final cone either sprint 10â€“20 meters at maximum speed, or finish without a sprint to focus on ball control.
  5. Rest briefly and repeat for 30â€“60 seconds per round.

Coaching Focus

  • Quick, sharp changes of direction during the zigzag.
  • Tight touches during the weave.
  • Use both feet and all surfaces of the foot.
  • Stay low with good balance.
  • Smooth transition from dribbling into acceleration while maintaining full control.

Variations

  • Right foot only / left foot only.
  • Increase or decrease cone distance.
  • Add a finishing move before the sprint (e.g., feint or turn).
  • Time each repetition and try to beat previous scores.
  • Add a ball recovery sprint (dribble to a certain point after the sprint).
