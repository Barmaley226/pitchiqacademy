Ball Control

Practical, space-friendly drills for all ages. Each drill includes steps, a quick tip, and a image or video with explanation.

Dribbling — 5 Drills

1. Basic Cone Weave

Session length: 5–10 minutes

Age group: All ages

Equipment: 1 ball, 6-10 cones

Skills developed: Ball control, dribbling, turning

Setup

  1. Use a 20-yard section of the field.
  2. Place the first cone to mark the starting point.
  3. Place a second cone 5 yards away to begin the weaving section.
  4. Set 5 cones 1 yard apart to form the weave.
  5. Place the final cone 5 yards beyond the last cone to finish the drill.
  6. All players start behind the first cone. For 6+ players, create a second identical course.

Instructions

  1. Player 1 (P1) starts by dribbling toward the cone weave.
  2. P1 dribbles in and out between the cones until completing the weaving section.
  3. After rounding the final cone, P1 passes to Player 2 (P2) and moves to the back of the line.
  4. P2 then repeats the drill. Players should focus on controlling the ball and avoiding cones.
  5. Gradually increase speed as players get comfortable.

Variations

  • Dribble using only the right foot.
  • Dribble using only the left foot.
  • Dribble weaving from right-to-left or left-to-right.
  • Introduce a timed challenge to see who completes the course fastest (penalties for losing control).

Tip: Keep short, frequent touches and stay light on your feet — shorten touches when increasing speed.

2. Zig-Zag Dribbling Drill

Session length: 5–10 minutes

Age group: All ages

Equipment: 1 ball per player, 6–8 cones

Skills developed: Ball control, dribbling with speed, using inside, outside, and sole of the foot

Setup

  1. Arrange cones in a zig-zag pattern about 5 yards apart.
  2. The total length of the course should be approximately 20 yards.
  3. Players start at the first cone, each with a ball.
  4. SIf the group is large, split into two lines with identical zig-zag setups.

Instructions

  1. The first player dribbles through the zig-zag, weaving around each cone.
  2. When reaching the end, sprint back with the ball to the starting line.
  3. Repeat the drill several times so players get comfortable controlling the ball while changing directions.

Coaching Points

  • Keep touches controlled and close.
  • Alternate between inside, outside, and sole of the foot.
  • Gradually increase dribbling speed without losing control.

3. Ronaldinho Box Drill

Session Length: 5–10 minutes

Age Group: All ages

Equipment: 1 ball per player, 16–20 small cones

Skills Developed: Ball control, footwork, agility, creativity

Drill Setup

  1. Arrange 16–20 cones in a 4×4 square grid (increase spacing for older players).
  2. Players start with a ball inside the square; if multiple players, take turns.

Instructions

  1. Move the ball around the cones using different parts of the foot: inside, outside, sole and toe.
  2. Keep touches tight and change direction frequently while moving creatively around the box.
  3. Use both feet and vary tempo; perform several 30–60s rounds with short rest between.
  4. Progress by increasing speed or adding a small competitive element (who completes most controlled moves).

Variations

  • Use only the dominant foot for one round, then only the weaker foot for another.
  • Include a short juggling sequence between touches for ball mastery.
  • Time each player and create a friendly challenge or require a minimum number of moves per round.
  • Challenge players to invent and perform a new move while keeping the ball in the box.

Coaching Points

  • Keep the ball close to maintain control; small quick touches work best.
  • Encourage creativity and fluid movement rather than large, wild kicks.
  • Use low center of gravity and keep head up regularly to develop spatial awareness.

Tip: Focus on rhythm and tempo first, then add speed — quality of touch over flashy moves.

4. Dribbling + Acceleration Drill

Session Length: 10–15 minutes

Age Group: All ages

Equipment: 1 ball, 8–9 cones

Skills Developed: Dribbling, footwork, acceleration, stamina

Drill Setup

  1. Place 4–5 cones in a zig-zag pattern, about 3–4m apart.
  2. After the zig-zag, set 4 cones in a straight line for a short cone weave.
  3. Mark a 10–20m sprint line after the last cone.
  4. Each player starts with a ball at the first zig-zag cone.

Drill Instructions

  1. Dribble through the zig-zag section with controlled touches.
  2. After the last zig-zag cone, move directly into the straight cone weave, keeping the ball close.
  3. When finished the weave, leave the ball at the last cone and accelerate into a 10–20m sprint to the finish line.
  4. Jog back, collect the ball, return to the start and rotate players smoothly to maintain intensity.

Coaching Points

  • Keep touches close and controlled while changing direction.
  • Focus on balance when turning around cones.
  • The sprint must be explosive — full acceleration; practice quick transitions from dribbling to running.

Variations

  • No sprint.
  • Add a timed challenge for more intensity.

Tip: Emphasize quality of touch during the dribble and maximum effort on the sprint.

Long Dribbling Drill

Session Length: 8–12 minutes

Age Group: 10+

Skills Developed: Speed dribbling, close control, acceleration, coordination

Equipment: 1 ball per player, 2 cones, 15–30 m space

Drill Setup

  1. Place two cones 3–4 meters apart as the starting gate.
  2. Mark a straight line of 15–30 meters for the dribble.
  3. Ensure each player has a ball at the start.

Drill Instructions

  1. Dribble through the two cones using short, controlled touches to start.
  2. Immediately after the cones, accelerate forward and dribble the full 15–30 meters at maximum speed.
  3. Keep the ball close using light, frequent touches and slow down at the end to reset.
  4. Reset and repeat for the desired number of reps or time.

Coaching Points

  • Keep the ball within playing distance at all times.
  • Maintain balance and steady posture; look up regularly.
  • Increase speed gradually without losing control.

Variations

  • Right foot only / left foot only.
  • Finish the run with a pass or a shot.

Tip: Focus on a compact touch when accelerating — control first, then add speed.

Receiving & First Touch — 4 Drills

1. Wall Control Progression

  1. Stand 4–6m from a wall. Pass the ball to the wall and receive the rebound.
  2. Control the ball with one touch and pass back; vary surface (inside, sole, thigh).
  3. Increase pass speed and vary angles as accuracy improves.

Tip: Cushion the ball by bending the knee and softening the contact.

2. Aerial Receive & Set

  1. Partner tosses balls of different heights from 5–8m.
  2. Receive using chest, thigh or foot and take a controlling touch to set up the next action.
  3. Repeat both feet and both sides of the body.

Tip: Watch the ball into your body and absorb pace with a flexible joint.

Video: Add video link

3. Gate One-Touch Control

  1. Set a small gate (1.2–1.5m). Partner plays passes through the gate to you.
  2. Control the ball with one touch and push it through the gate to a target or next player.
  3. Increase pass speed and vary pass height over time.

Tip: Angle your body so the first touch directs the ball where you want to go next.

Video: Add video link

4. Moving Control Routine

  1. Walk or jog across 10–12m while partner plays passes at varying speeds and heights.
  2. Receive on the run and take a quality touch that keeps momentum.
  3. Switch directions and repeat for both feet.

Tip: Open your body early to face the play and reduce wasted touches.

Passing & Technique (Wall / Partner) — 4 Drills

1. Wall One-Touch Series

  1. Stand 4–6m from a wall. Play one-touch passes and receive the rebound with one touch back to the wall.
  2. Aim for controlled tempo bursts of 30–60s followed by rest.

Tip: Focus on body positioning and planting the support foot for consistent weight.

Video: Add video link

2. Wall Control + Through Pass

  1. Pass to the wall, control the rebound, then play a driven pass through a gate 10–15m away.
  2. Alternate left/right foot and adjust pass weight to hit the gate consistently.

Tip: Use the inside for accuracy and the laces for longer driven passes.

Video: Add video link

3. Partner Short Passing & Move

  1. Pair up and stand 5–8m apart. Pass and follow your pass to create new angles.
  2. Use one- and two-touch combinations for 1–2 minutes, then increase tempo or reduce distance.

Tip: Communicate and open your body to receive on the half-turn for quicker play.

Video: Add video link

4. Target Passing Challenge

  1. Place small targets (cones or marked areas) at varying distances (10–30m).
  2. Partner tries to hit targets with driven or lofted passes; score points for accuracy.
  3. Rotate roles and vary target distance each round.

Tip: Focus on follow-through and body alignment toward the target.
