Long Dribbling Drill

Session Length: 5â€“10 minutes

Age Group: All ages

Skills Developed: Long-distance dribbling, ball control at speed, coordination, acceleration, stamina

Equipment: 1 ball, 4-6 cones

Space Needed: Up to 30 meters

Setup

  • Place 2 cones about 3â€“5 meters apart at the starting area.
  • Place another 2 cones 20â€“30 meters away to mark the finish line.
  • The total dribbling distance should be up to 30 meters.

Instructions

  1. Start with the ball at the first two cones.
  2. Dribble around both cones in a controlled loop (figure-8 or circle), keeping tight touches.
  3. Immediately accelerate forward into a long dribble toward the cones at the end.
  4. Use short, fast touches while sprinting to keep control at high speed.
  5. Reach the final cones, stop the ball, rest shortly, and repeat.

Coaching Focus

  • Smooth transition from tight dribbling into full sprint.
  • Short, controlled touches while running at top speed.
  • Ball always close enough to adjust direction.
  • Stay balanced and keep the body slightly forward.
  • Eyes scanning forward, not only on the ball.
  • Max effort over the final 20â€“30 meters.

Variations

  • Use only one foot for the tight dribbling section.
  • Increase sprint distance up to 30 meters.
  • Time each run for competition.
  • Add a turn at the finish cone and dribble back.
  • Add a feint before starting the long sprint.
